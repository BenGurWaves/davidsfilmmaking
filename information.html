Bio

David Ivanyuck is a filmmaker driven by an instinct to transform fleeting thoughts into images that linger. His practice weaves between experimental visuals, grounded narrative, and the unpredictable collisions between the two. He approaches film as both craft and diary, using motion pictures to translate the rhythm of his days into a language of light and shadow.

Born with a restless curiosity, he cut his teeth on short-form experiments before challenging himself to build larger works—projects that demand endurance, vision, and failure in equal measure. For David, art is less about arriving at mastery and more about surviving the stretch between doubt and discovery. Each film is an artifact of that survival.

His influences are broad—painters, novelists, internet oddities—and each one leaves a trace. But what defines his work isn’t homage; it’s honesty. David builds films that acknowledge their own process, the fingerprints left behind, the fractures that hold the image together.

Archive

Beyond the polished projects lie dozens of unfinished fragments: tests of light, experiments in editing, collaborations that collapsed into memory. The archive is both a record and a playground—proof that process matters as much as final product.

Short films, essays, installations, and photo series all live here in various forms. Some are rough sketches, some are nearly complete. The archive reminds both artist and audience that film is alive, never finished, and always in conversation with itself.
