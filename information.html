Bio

I am David Ivanyuck, a filmmaker and visual artist whose work straddles the line between experimental cinema and narrative storytelling. My approach is rooted in texture, rhythm, and emotion rather than linear progression. Every frame is crafted as if it could stand alone as a photograph, while together they form an audiovisual poem.

My journey began not in film school, but through late nights experimenting with cameras, editing, and projection. This autodidact path gave me a unique visual language—one that embraces imperfection, noise, and grain as living, breathing elements in storytelling.

Today, I collaborate with musicians, brands, and other artists to create work that moves fluidly between commercial and personal, digital and analog, chaos and order.

Contact

Interested in collaboration, commissions, or just a conversation? I’m always open to connecting with creatives who share a love for film, photography, or experimental media. Whether it’s a brand campaign, music video, or an independent art project, I’d love to hear your ideas.

Please reach out via email at contact@davidivanyuck.com. You can also find me sharing fragments, works-in-progress, and inspiration across social platforms like Instagram and Vimeo.

Let’s build something that challenges, disturbs, and inspires.

Archive

My archive is a living organism—short films, visual studies, personal experiments, and commissioned work all coexisting in dialogue. It is less of a gallery and more of a diary: a place where polished projects meet raw sketches.

Each piece is part of a continuum. Sometimes I revisit old footage years later, cutting it into new shapes, layering it with different sounds, or projecting it onto unconventional surfaces. The archive is where I experiment freely, unbound by deadlines or expectations.

Think of it as a sandbox—messy, alive, and constantly shifting.
